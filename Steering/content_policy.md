Violations of Acceptable Use. 
The following are examples of, but are not limited to, inappropriate use of school-provided devices, networks, the internet, or other online services and are not permitted on the part of USD 259 staff and students: 
1) Accessing, uploading, downloading, or distributing pornographic, obscene, threatening/harassing/bullying, or sexually explicit material and/or language; 
2) Violating any local, state, or federal statute; 
3) Violating copyright or otherwise using the intellectual property of another individual or organization without permission; 
4) Accessing another individual's materials, information, passwords or files without permission; 
5) “Hacking” and other illegal activities in an attempt to gain unauthorized access to restricted files, other devices or computer systems. Uploading any harmful form of programming, bypassing filters, installing any type of server, aliasing/spoofing, peer-to-peer networking, or remote-control software; 
6) Vandalizing, defined as any unauthorized access and/or malicious attempt to damage device hardware/software or networks or destroying the data of another user, including creating, uploading, or intentionally introducing viruses; 
7) Intentionally wasting limited network or bandwidth resources; 
8) Using the network or e-mail lists for commercial purposes or profit; 
9) Using, disclosing, or disseminating personal information online such as full name, home address, phone number, etc., except with approval by certified or administrative district staff; 
10) Gaining unauthorized access to resources or entities; 
11) Invading the privacy of individuals; 
12) Improperly altering the setup of devices (e.g., desktops, icons, wallpapers, screensavers, installed software) as determined by the network administrator; 
13) Using software/apps that have not been assigned or approved by the appropriate staff; 
14) Failing to follow a district policy while using devices or failing to follow any other policies or guidelines established by district administration, teachers, or other appropriate district staff; and 
15) Seeking to gain or gaining unauthorized access to information resources or other computing devices.
